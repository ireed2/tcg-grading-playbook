03
Corners, Edges & Surface
Answer yes or no for each item — be honest, graders see everything
Get set up before you start Hold a flashlight or lamp low and to the SIDE of your card so the light skims across the surface at a shallow angle. Look through a magnifying glass if you have one. Rotate the card slowly as you examine each area.
Corners — check all four corners front and back
Do all four corner tips look sharp and pointed — not rounded or flat? Look closely at the very tip of each corner. A rounded tip means the corner has wear.
Under your magnifying glass — do you see tiny thread-like fibers at any corner tip? Like a frayed piece of fabric at the very edge. Even one tiny fiber costs points.
Can you see any crease lines running from a corner toward the center of the card? These often feel slightly raised or depressed when you run a finger gently near the corner.
Edges — look straight down each of the four edges
Does your card have dark-colored borders? Dark borders (like many modern Pokémon cards) show edge damage much more clearly than white borders.
When you look straight down each edge — does every edge look smooth and straight? Any bumps, waves, or rough spots are edge damage.
Are there any tiny chips or nicks — small pieces missing from any edge? Even a tiny missing chip on a dark border will be caught by graders under magnification.
On the dark borders — do you see any white marks, scratches, or scuffs along any edge? White marks on dark borders are one of the most common and punishing defects for modern cards.
Surface — front and back
Tilt the shiny part of the front slowly under your light — do any thin scratch lines appear and disappear? Go slowly through a full half-circle. Holo scratches are nearly invisible straight-on but catch light at specific angles.
Does the shiny surface look clear and bright — no foggy, cloudy, or dull areas anywhere? Haziness on the holo layer is a significant defect even if the card looks fine otherwise.
Hold the card back almost flat to your light — do you see any dull white patches on the back surface? These pressure marks or "whitening" look less shiny than the rest of the back. Very common from normal storage.
Do you see any thin parallel lines running across the back — like marks from stacking with other cards? These are called stacking marks and are caught by graders even when they look very faint.
